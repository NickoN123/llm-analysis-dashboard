Executive Summary

Ted McConnell is a highly respected advertising and marketing measurement innovator, whose nearly 32-year career at Procter & Gamble was defined by leading digital strategy, innovation, and measurement thinking for one of the world's most influential consumer brands. After retiring from P&G, he became Executive Vice President—Digital at the Advertising Research Foundation (ARF), helping the industry rethink measurement quality, engagement metrics, and the context in which digital marketing is evaluated.

Surprising Strength

A consistent focus on truth and measurement integrity, helping shift industry standards toward meaningful outcomes beyond simple reach metrics.

Surprising Weakness

His emphasis on deep analytical rigor and quality measurement can sometimes appear esoteric or removed from fast-paced tactical execution.

Brand Essence & Character

Ted McConnell's executive brand embodies rigor, clarity, and accountability in how marketing performance is assessed. Rather than accepting commonplace metrics at face value, he challenges the industry to ask: "Are we measuring what matters—attention, context, and true engagement?" His work at ARF emphasized this drive toward holistic understanding and meaningful standards.

He represents a guardian of marketing truth, focused less on short-term optimization and more on quality, context, and long-term accountability in advertising measurement.

Why this matters: His career arc—from building digital capabilities at P&G to shaping measurement innovation at ARF—reflects a deep alignment with where marketing must evolve next: from impressions to meaning.

Executive Presence Type: The Wise Educator

Ted McConnell's presence in the industry—historically through keynote talks, thought leadership publications (e.g., Media Insider, MediaPost), and measurement forums—projects intellectual authority grounded in practical marketing reality. His visual and public influence spans years of speaking engagements and industry debates.

Observed characteristics:
  • No social media noise: McConnell is far less performative than many modern marketing leaders, instead favoring depth of insight over flash.
  • A professor-like presence: He speaks in analytical narratives, makes nuanced distinctions between quality and quantity in media, and elevates discourse beyond surface KPIs.
  • Authentic legacy: His long tenure at P&G and later at ARF aligns with credibility and steadfast commitment to industry betterment rather than personal brand self-promotion.

Why this matters: His visibility is less about selfies or real-time posts and more about industry hauling the conversation into higher ground—toward measurement truth and research stewardship.

Final Assessment

Ted McConnell stands as a wise guardian of measurement integrity in the advertising ecosystem—a leader who insists that what we measure must matter and that context and attention quality are central to true effectiveness. His leadership legacy blends data rigor, thoughtful innovation, and stewardship over industry measurement practices. McConnell's impact may be less visible in flashy social posts, but in deep, enduring conversations about what counts, he remains irreplaceable.
