Arts & Media Programs
Occidental — Media Arts & Culture (MAC)
MAC blends critical theory, film production, documentary, animation, AR/VR, and digital design across two tracks: Critical Media and Media Production. LA location means studio internships are part of the curriculum, not extras. Cross-listed with music and CS — Film Scoring, Haptic Media — reflect genuine integration. Faculty are active professional filmmakers and scholars.
AlsoStudio Art, Theater, Music, Dance, IDW Writing
BonusArt Center College of Design cross-enrollment
UCSC — Film & Digital Media (FDM)
25-year-old nationally respected department. Graduates have screened at Sundance, Toronto, and on HBO. New Social Documentation Lab (2024) has state-of-the-art editing, color grading, and immersive media facilities. Multiple Guggenheim Fellows on faculty. Rigorous blend of film history, theory, and hands-on production. PhD and MFA programs raise the intellectual floor for undergrads.
AlsoArt BA/BFA, Theater, Music, Game Design
RankingTop 5 nationally in game/simulation development
Emerson — School of Film, Television & Media Arts (NEW 2025)
Elevated to its own dedicated School in 2025 — recognition of Emerson's largest and most defining program. Offers Media Arts Production BA/BFA, Film Art BFA (including a Paris global track with Paris College of Art), and the pioneering Media Psychology BS. New courses in 2025 include Immersive Media in the AI Era, Virtual Production, Music Video Production, and Accessible Cinema. Emerson LA campus on Sunset Blvd is an award-winning industry-connected facility.
AlsoPerforming Arts, Writing/Lit/Publishing, Journalism, Comedic Arts BFA

USF — Media Studies BA
USF's Media Studies program trains students to both analyze and produce media — critique, audio, video, and journalism — within a Jesuit social justice framework. The USF Media Lab is open to production course students for film, podcast, and video creation. Student work is archived in the USF MediaZine with alumni profiles. Past internships have included local TV and radio stations, film festivals, social change organizations, and social media firms. Faculty include a GRAMMY member audio engineer, an Emmy-winning broadcast creative, and a filmmaker whose work is in the MOMA and Library of Congress.

Psychology Programs
Occidental — Psychology
Strong liberal arts psych focused on writing, research methods, and critical thinking. No grad program means undergrads get full faculty attention — real research participation, not assistant roles. LA's clinical, nonprofit, and entertainment psychology sectors provide genuine internship pipelines. Natural bridge to MAC for media-psychology intersections.
UCSC — Psychology
Full research university dept with active labs in developmental, social, cognitive, and clinical neuroscience. Students who identify research areas early can co-publish with faculty — a major PhD credential. Silicon Valley proximity creates pathways into UX research and behavioral analytics. The environment rewards students who take initiative and seek out faculty directly.
Emerson — Media Psychology BS
Emerson's true differentiator for Sophia. The Media Psychology BS is one of the only undergraduate programs in the country designed around the intersection of both fields — studying how media shapes human behavior, cognition, emotion, and culture. If she's interested in both psychology and media, this isn't a compromise; it's the most intellectually direct path of all four schools. Production courses are literally down the hallway.
USF — Psychology BA
Comprehensive liberal arts psychology in the Jesuit tradition. Covers social, biological, clinical, cultural, developmental, and ethical dimensions of human behavior. Community-engaged learning is built into the degree — students gain hands-on experience in Bay Area hospitals, mental health centers, and community organizations. Strong pipeline into USF's own graduate psychology programs and SF's rich clinical sector. Students frequently note faculty are genuinely accessible and personally invested.

Things that don't always come up in standard college conversations — but matter enormously in practice.
UCSC: Which of the 10 residential colleges?
College assignment shapes your entire UCSC social world — dining, events, advising, and first-year core course. Every undergrad is affiliated regardless of major. Express preferences during admissions. Two are especially relevant for Sophia:
Porter College — dedicated to the arts; has galleries, studio spaces, and an arts-forward identity built into daily programming. The natural home for an arts/media student.
Cowell College (est. 1965) — the founding UCSC college. Motto: "The Pursuit of Truth in the Company of Friends." Home to the Eloise Pickard Smith Gallery and the Cowell Press. Where Sophia's dad studied. Walking Cowell with him on the visit is worth planning for.
USF: San Francisco is genuinely part of the curriculum
USF's location isn't just a perk — it's built into how students learn. The Community Engaged Learning requirement places students into real Bay Area organizations: media companies, nonprofits, hospitals, arts institutions, and social enterprises. Media Studies students have interned at local TV and radio stations, film festivals, and social media firms. Psychology students work in Bay Area mental health centers and community organizations. And the neighbors include Lucasfilm, ILM, and American Zoetrope. Very few colleges in the country can say that the film industry is a two-mile walk from the dorm. The city — its diversity, its creative energy, its social complexity — is a living classroom that no curriculum could replicate.
Emerson: Does focusing entirely on one field excite or limit her?
Emerson is the most specialized school in this group — almost everything is communication, arts, or media. Students who thrive love the shared creative language; everyone speaks the same dialect. Students who struggle want intellectual diversity — the freedom to take a philosophy or biology elective "just to see." If she has any doubt about media/arts as her core, the other three schools offer more breadth. If she's certain, Emerson's focus is a genuine superpower.
Emerson's Media Psychology BS: worth a serious look
If Sophia is genuinely drawn to both media and psychology, the Media Psychology BS is one of the only undergraduate programs in the country designed around their intersection. She wouldn't need to build a bridge between two separate majors — the bridge is the program. Research the curriculum and ask to speak with a current Media Psychology student before deciding. This might be the single most compelling argument for Emerson in Sophia's specific case.
Housing realities — all four schools
Oxy: On-campus housing guaranteed all 4 years — a major quality-of-life advantage and community anchor. UCSC: Guaranteed only freshman year; off-campus Santa Cruz is expensive and competitive. Emerson: Required freshman year; Boston off-campus is pricey but manageable. USF: Only ~38% live on campus — most upperclassmen live in SF neighborhoods. Research current housing costs in each city carefully before deciding.
Weather and daily life texture
This matters more than applicants expect. Oxy/LA: warm, sunny, sprawling — a car eventually helps. UCSC: coastal fog, stunning nature, self-contained campus. Emerson/Boston: real winters (November through March), culturally rich, fully walkable and transit-connected — but cold. USF/SF: Bay Area microclimates, often cool and foggy, beautiful city, walkable campus, Golden Gate Park right next door.
Career center depth in her specific fields
Oxy: Strong LA entertainment/media connections; loyal alumni network. UCSC: Strong in tech, SV digital media; growing in arts. Emerson: Deep Boston + LA media pipelines; film/TV/journalism alumni network is exceptional. USF: Strong SF tech, nonprofit, and social sector — faculty with active industry credits like Emmy-winner Jeremy Troy and GRAMMY-member Rajeev Naidu create real professional bridges. Ask each school: "What did a recent arts or media student do for their first summer internship?"
Mental health and wellness infrastructure
Worth researching everywhere. UCSC's CAPS is free to all registered students, no insurance needed. Emerson Wellness Center provides counseling, therapy groups, and psychiatric consultation. USF and Oxy both have counseling services. Ask each: what's the average wait time for a first appointment? Given Sophia's interest in psychology, how a campus treats its students' mental health is itself educational.
How to use this: The goal isn't to collect answers — it's to notice how people respond. Enthusiasm, hesitation, and evasion all tell you something real. Bring a notebook. Trust your gut more than any ranking.
