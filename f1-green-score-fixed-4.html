Formula One Green Score Leaderboard 2026

Prepared by Leila Blackshaw-Brown, Student: University of St. Andrews

I'm an economics and sustainable development student—and a lifelong Formula 1 fan—committed to environmental responsibility. I created this scorecard to challenge the sport I love to match its reputation for innovation with accountability for its environmental and social impact. The Green Score evaluates drivers across five dimensions: Mobility Efficiency, Lifestyle Footprint, Mitigation Credibility, Values Consistency, and Impact on Formula 1's sustainability transformation. Hover over headers for methodology details or cells for driver analysis. I'm committed to making this a highly credible index—please send feedback to poletoplanet@gmail.com.

Creator's Note on Methodology

I reviewed data accessible from all major answer engines but anchored most of the analysis to Anthropic's Claude tool. Key sources "blended" into the scorecard include:

  • The Guardian's environmental reporting on Formula 1 and driver sustainability initiatives
  • Formula 1's official sustainability reports and Net Zero carbon roadmap
  • FIA environmental policy documentation and driver conduct records
  • Driver social media accounts and public statements on environmental issues
  • Extreme E racing series documentation (Hamilton ownership)
  • Mission 44 foundation environmental program reports
  • Verified carbon offset registries and renewable energy investment databases
  • European real estate carbon footprint assessments for Monaco and UK residences
  • Private aviation carbon calculator databases
  • Plant-based diet carbon footprint research from Oxford University
  • Sports personality platform influence studies
  • Team sustainability reports from Mercedes, Ferrari, Red Bull, McLaren, and Alpine
