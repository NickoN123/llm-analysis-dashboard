Deep Intelligence — Qualitative Analysis
What AI Engines Think About Oral Care
Beyond naming brands, we asked six AI engines to reason through a range of open-ended topics — including brand trust architecture, safety vs. effectiveness tradeoffs, ingredient transparency, the role of influencers and credentialing, and what it would take to win in an AI-driven Answer Economy. We're not publishing the question set. But here's what the engines told us — and what it means for brands.
What the Engines Said — Direct
Trust & Credibility Architecture
"A default recommendation is built on safety, efficacy, accessibility, and familiarity. Dentists recommend what they were trained with, what patients recognize, and what has consistent evidence. Colgate occupies all three simultaneously — making it structurally the default."
— ChatGPT
Safety vs. Effectiveness Tradeoffs
"The biggest misconception is that 'natural' automatically means safer. Safety depends on dose, formulation, abrasivity, and evidence — not whether ingredients sound familiar or synthetic. Brands that conflate purity with proof are making a bet the data doesn't support."
— Claude
Trust & Credibility Architecture
"Sensodyne built unwavering credibility by focusing exclusively on a specific medical need and delivering consistently on that promise. It didn't try to win every question — it owned one, completely. That specificity became its structural moat."
— Gemini
Influencers & Credentialing
"Dentists remain the highest-trust voice in oral care — but their influence now travels through YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit as much as the exam room. The brands winning credentialing aren't just sponsoring clinicians; they're giving clinicians something worth talking about."
— Grok
AI & the Future of Brand Discovery
"AI answer engines compress the market toward default winners — favoring brands with dense clinical evidence, strong authority signals, and consistent presence across search, professional sources, and reviews. The new moat is being the cited answer, not the advertised option."
— Perplexity
Ingredient Transparency
"Consumers now scan toothpaste labels the way they scan food labels. Brands that proactively explain what each ingredient does — not just list it — are building a durable trust advantage that advertising cannot replicate and that AI training data will reward for years."
— DeepSeek
Where the Engines Diverge — Qualitative Personality
ChatGPT — The Consensus Builder
Tends toward the authoritative mainstream answer. Rarely surfaces nuance, controversy, or challenger perspectives. In qualitative questions, ChatGPT reinforces the existing hierarchy — Colgate is trustworthy, dentists are authoritative, clinical evidence rules. Brands that want a predictable ChatGPT answer should invest in professional endorsement density and ADA signals.
Claude — The Scrutinizer
The only engine that consistently flagged safety risks, ingredient controversies, and the limits of mainstream brand claims without being asked. Claude named PFAS in Glide, SLS concerns in Crest, and challenged "natural = safe" more forcefully than any other engine. For brands, Claude's answer is the hardest to earn and the most valuable to hold.
Grok — The Cultural Lens
Most attuned to how credentialing actually travels in 2026 — through YouTube dental creators, TikTok hygienists, and Reddit oral care communities as much as dental offices. Grok's qualitative answers reflect the social reality that dentist authority has gone multichannel. Brands that give clinicians something worth talking about online are winning the credentialing game; brands that only run traditional sampling programs are losing it without realizing it.
Gemini — The Editorial Mirror
Qualitative answers closely mirror the structure and framing of health publisher editorial — Healthline, WebMD, Cleveland Clinic. Gemini describes trust in the same language these sites use. Brands with strong editorial coverage on these platforms have a direct pipeline to Gemini's qualitative reasoning, making editorial placement a first-order AI strategy.
Perplexity — The Source Authority
Most likely to cite specific institutional sources in qualitative answers — ADA guidelines, dental association position papers, indexed journal abstracts. Perplexity's qualitative reasoning is the most citation-anchored of any engine. Brands that want to influence Perplexity's qualitative outputs must invest upstream in the sources Perplexity trusts, not just consumer-facing content.
DeepSeek — The Evidence Counter
Qualitative answers are the most data-anchored of the six engines — frequently referencing market share figures, clinical trial counts, and publication volume. DeepSeek's reasoning for why a brand is trustworthy tends to be quantitative: "most clinical research," "highest market penetration," "largest ADA portfolio." The engine most rewarding for brands that publish rigorously.
Eight Dominant Themes from the Qualitative Runs
THEME 01
Colgate owns the default — but it also owns the risk
Every engine named Colgate as the brand with the strongest long-term credibility, broadest professional endorsement, and most consistent ADA recognition. Yet the same engines also note Colgate's exposure to safety scrutiny over artificial dyes, SLS, and ingredient complexity. Colgate is simultaneously the most recommended brand and the most pressure-tested — the Coca-Cola dynamic of oral care.
Colgate Safety exposure Unanimous 6/6
THEME 02
Dentists are the single most powerful authority channel
All six engines independently cited the dental professional relationship as the primary trust driver — more than advertising, retail presence, or social proof. Brands that built their authority through dental school sampling programs, professional detailing, and ADA Seal of Acceptance have encoded a form of trust that AI replicates because it derives from the most cited health authority sources in training data.
Colgate Oral-B Professional channel
THEME 03
Safety scrutiny is reshaping the category in real time
Five of six engines flagged ingredient safety as a defining trend reshaping consumer perception — specifically concerns about SLS, artificial sweeteners, microplastics, and PFAS in floss. Brands previously considered unassailable (Crest, Oral-B Glide) are now named in AI answers as exposure risks. The AI training data has absorbed the investigative journalism and peer-reviewed research on these issues — brands cannot outrun this signal with advertising alone.
Crest Oral-B Glide PFAS / SLS signal
THEME 04
Bite and David's are building the clean-label future
Four engines named Bite as the most sustainable oral care brand — a striking consensus for a challenger brand. David's, Boka, and Georganics collectively represent a new architecture of brand authority: ingredient transparency, circular packaging, and clean-label positioning built for consumers who treat toothpaste labels the same way they treat food labels. These brands are disproportionately present in AI answers relative to their market share.
Bite David's Boka Clean-label wave
THEME 05
The natural / effective tradeoff is the core consumer tension
Every engine described the same underlying tension: natural brands offer ingredient purity but sacrifice clinical proof on hard endpoints like cavity prevention. Clinically effective brands deliver on evidence but face consumer skepticism about synthetic ingredients. Sensodyne and Tom's of Maine were most frequently cited as brands that successfully bridge this gap — one through clinical rigor, the other through ADA-accepted natural formulas. The tension isn't resolving; brands must pick a position and defend it.
Sensodyne Tom's of Maine Bridge brands
THEME 06
AI answer engines are compressing oral care choices into five brands
Across 110 questions, five brands — Colgate, Oral-B, Sensodyne, Listerine, Parodontax — dominate the AI answers in a way that mirrors how Google's first page compressed web discovery. Brands not in this cohort face an existential visibility challenge: the Answer Economy doesn't have a page 2. The engines that consumers consult increasingly return the same five names regardless of query variation, making it harder for challengers to earn share of AI recommendation.
Colgate Oral-B Sensodyne Listerine The Big Five
THEME 07
Oral-B is uniquely positioned for the connected health future
Five engines named Oral-B as the brand most likely to define next-generation oral care — specifically because of its AI brushing coaching, app integration, position-detection technology, and data ownership model. The iO Series represents the first oral care product that creates a recurring data relationship with the consumer. In an AI-driven health ecosystem, the brand that owns the brushing data owns the recommendation infrastructure.
Oral-B iO Connected health future Data ownership
THEME 08
The AI-first oral care brand doesn't exist yet
When asked what it would take to win the AI-driven Answer Economy, every engine described the same architecture: published clinical trials, ADA or equivalent validation, structured health authority content, dentist advocacy density, and ingredient transparency that withstands AI-powered scrutiny. No single brand was named as having fully achieved all five. Colgate comes closest on authority; Boka and Bite come closest on transparency. The brand that merges both will own the next decade of AI-driven oral care recommendations.
Boka Bite AI-first architecture Legacy brands at risk

ABOUT THIS STUDY
BrandRank.AI Oral Care Sector Diagnostic — March 2026
110 quantitative brand authority questions were submitted identically to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity, and DeepSeek in a controlled session during March 2026. Answers were recorded verbatim and coded for brand assignment. Each question required a single primary brand answer — reflecting how consumers experience AI recommendations in real decision moments. The resulting 660 data points represent the most comprehensive cross-engine oral care brand authority study conducted to date. Questions span 11 pillars: Core Toothpaste, Toothbrush, Flossing, Mouthwash, Whitening, Gum Health, Breath & Freshness, Kids & Family, Natural & Sustainable, Innovation & Future, and Authority & Discovery.
