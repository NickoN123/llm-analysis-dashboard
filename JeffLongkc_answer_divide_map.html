Answer Divide℠ — Scoring Glossary & Framework Guide
The Answer Divide℠ measures how well a brand’s digital presence serves a curious consumer — someone who arrives with a question before a purchase intent. The goal isn’t to replace conversion; it’s to earn the trust that makes conversion happen naturally. Brands that answer questions well become the ones AI models draw from and recommend. Both KC and P&G brands are scored on an identical rubric.
AFO
Above-the-Fold Orientation
What does the hero communicate on arrival? 5 = consumer question or empathy signal. 3 = concern and product equally weighted. 1 = pure product or CTA hero.
NAV
Navigation Architecture
Is the site organised around what the consumer needs to know or what the brand sells? 5 = 100% need-led. 3 = hybrid. 1 = pure product navigation.
EDU
Educational Content Depth
How deep and expert is the content library? 5 = standalone expert content navigable without purchase intent. 3 = moderate, partly funnel-embedded. 1 = product descriptions only.
AID
Answer Independence
Can a consumer get a complete answer without selecting a product? 5 = fully independent throughout. 3 = accessible but product always adjacent. 1 = every answer requires product selection.
CONV
Conversion Pressure (inverted)
Purchase pressure across the full experience. 5 = content-first, no visible purchase pressure. 3 = moderate CTAs. 1 = every interaction routes aggressively to buy.

Key Observations · KC vs. P&G
Sensitive categories: P&G has invested deeper
Both portfolios follow the same pattern — stronger curiosity investment in sensitive need categories (baby, feminine, adult care), lighter investment in paper products. The difference is depth. Pampers (22) leads Huggies (19) in navigation architecture and content richness. Always (20) leads Kotex (14) with life-stage education and empowerment content. The strategic orientation is the same; P&G has simply gone further within it.
Paper products: an open opportunity for both
Bounty (10), Scott (6), and Viva (6) all sit in the lower half of the chart. Neither P&G nor KC has made a meaningful curiosity investment in paper products. This is actually the clearest opportunity in the map — the brand that builds genuine household utility content in this category first will own the answer layer for it, and the baseline is very low on both sides.
