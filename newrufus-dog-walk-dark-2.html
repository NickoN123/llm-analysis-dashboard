Serving A Great Report

Amazon recently reported that Rufus generated $10 billion in incremental revenue and that 60% of all users purchased more. Increasingly, BrandRank clients want supplemental Rufus perspective to inform their overall perspective on the role and impact of answer engines. This exercise meets that requirement without overburdening our analyst or tech platform with extra work.

By following this guide, you'll conduct a Rufus Answer Share Pulse Check (quarterly or monthly) using simple, pre-prepared templates that you can customize as needed. While the tool is called "The 20 Minute Dog Walk," the actual execution time is 20-30 minutes for running queries. Total time including setup and reporting is typically 45-60 minutes.

These reports are intended to be interactive and user-friendly, allowing clients to simply wave the cursor over certain sections for deeper analysis. Send feedback to help us improve.

2. Prep

1 Prepare Your Prompts

Take the standard 200 prompts and condense them strategically:

Parameter Specification
Total Prompts 60 prompts
Categories 6-8 different categories
Question Length 6-8 words (optimal for Rufus)
Order Most obvious to more specific questions

Prompt Optimization

Tightening questions to 6-8 words is ideal for Rufus. Shorter, more direct questions tend to produce cleaner, more comparable results.

3. Cooking

1 Load Your Prompts

Drop all 60 prompts into the Dog Walker tool interface.

Critical: Do Not Multitask

While Dog Walker is running continuous prompts, do not multitask on your computer. This can disrupt the data collection process. Best practices:

  • Run this before you sleep
  • Run during a lunch or coffee break
  • Schedule during downtime when you don't need your computer

2 Execute Queries

Press "Execute Queries" in the Dog Walker interface.

Expected Duration: 20-30 minutes

3 Extract Results

When the process completes:

  • Step 1: Carefully highlight ALL content that appears in the Rufus bar
  • Step 2: Cut and paste the content into a Word document
  • Step 3: Save the Word document
  • Step 4: Convert to PDF with a memorable name

File Size Expectations

Your final document could be 100+ pages depending on font size. This is normal and expected for comprehensive Rufus analysis.

Troubleshooting

Report doesn't look as expected

▼

Solution:

  • Upload a report you like (even from another brand or category)
  • Instruct Claude to ensure the new report has the same "look and feel"
  • Emphasize that the data should be preserved while matching the style

This approach allows you to maintain consistency across reports while customizing for specific analyses.

Dog Walker isn't running prompts

▼

Common Causes:

  • Extension not properly installed or enabled
  • Rufus not open in the Amazon tab
  • Browser interference from other extensions
  • Chrome not updated to latest version

Try:

  • Refresh the Amazon page
  • Disable other Chrome extensions temporarily
  • Check the Slack channel for recent troubleshooting updates

Results are incomplete or cut off

▼

Solution:

  • Make sure you highlighted ALL content in the Rufus bar before copying
  • Scroll to the very top and bottom to ensure complete capture
  • If needed, re-run specific queries that appear incomplete
  • Check that your Word document saved the full content before converting to PDF

Claude report generation fails

▼

Possible Issues:

  • PDF file too large for upload
  • Insufficient Claude account capacity
  • Template file missing or corrupted

Solutions:

  • Break large PDFs into smaller sections if needed
  • Ensure you're using Enterprise Claude for best results
  • Verify both template and data files are uploaded correctly

Prompts taking longer than 30 minutes

▼

This can happen when:

  • Amazon servers are experiencing high traffic
  • Your internet connection is slow
  • Rufus is processing complex queries

Recommendations:

  • Let it continue running - it should complete eventually
  • Check your internet connection stability
  • Try running during off-peak hours (early morning or late evening)

I want this as a PowerPoint, PDF, or Word document

▼

You are free to get creative with the data in any way you want. These particular reports are designed to be super user-friendly and even allow data to show up when you put the cursor over elements. But you can decide however you want to present the information.
