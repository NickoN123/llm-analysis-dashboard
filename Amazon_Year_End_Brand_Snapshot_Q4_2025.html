Essential Terms

AI Visibility — "Do I show up?" Measures how often and prominently your brand appears when consumers ask AI answer engines category-relevant questions. Score of 7+ means AI consistently recommends you; below 4 means you're invisible.

Brand Vulnerability — "What does AI say about me?" Measures whether AI is protecting or undermining your reputation—including accuracy of claims, sentiment, and competitive positioning.

Brand Promise Alignment — "Does AI believe my story?" Measures how convincingly AI engines communicate your core value proposition. A score below 7 means AI hedges or qualifies rather than endorses your promise.

Content Readiness — "Is my site ready for AI?" Measures whether AI can find, understand, and extract your content to use in answers. Broken into Availability (can AI find it), Depth (is it authoritative), and Clarity (can AI parse it).

Source Influence — "Who is shaping my AI narrative?" Identifies which sources AI cites when answering questions about your brand. High owned-source percentage means greater control over your narrative.

Engine Breakdown — AI engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Meta AI, Grok) use different training data and algorithms, so brand perception varies across platforms. ChatGPT is typically the primary consumer touchpoint.

Why Check Your Dashboard Regularly

AI models update frequently. Major platforms release updates that can shift your scores overnight. A visibility win in October can become a gap in November.

Competitors are moving. Your dashboard tracks competitive positioning in real-time. See competitor gains in scores before you see them in market share.

Prepare for key moments. Before earnings calls, board meetings, or campaign launches, check how AI currently represents your brand.

Catch misinformation early. AI can amplify inaccurate information from news cycles or Wikipedia edits. Regular monitoring catches issues before they spread.
