Key Insights & Trends

What the data reveals about AI recommendations in reverse mortgages

Precise Market Leaders

Finance of America: 33 positive mentions, 2 negative mentions across all 25 prompts, making it the most recommended overall despite Gemini's concerns about reviews and consumer complaints.

Longbridge Financial: 26 positive, 1 negative mention - consistently strong in trust and safety categories with minimal risk flags.

American Advisors Group (AAG): 22 positive, 4 negative mentions - high brand recognition but significant negative exposure in cost and regulatory categories.

Mutual of Omaha: 16 positive, 0 negative mentions - spotless negative record but narrower recommendation scope.

Consensus Spectrum

Highest consensus: "Most flexible terms" achieves 83% agreement on Finance of America (5/6 models), followed by "Top lenders" at 67% consensus (4/6 models).

Moderate consensus: Several prompts show 50% agreement, including "Best company overall" and "Best for existing mortgage" situations.

Lowest consensus: "Best reviews" shows only 17% agreement with 6 different recommendations, while negative categories average just 25-33% consensus, indicating vastly different risk assessment criteria.

Risk Profile Analysis

High-risk patterns: Reverse Mortgage Funding LLC flagged in 3 out of 5 negative categories, while Reverse Mortgage Solutions appears in 2 negative categories consistently.

AAG contradiction: Despite 22 positive recommendations, AAG appears in 4 negative mentions across "trust," "expensive," and "regulatory" categories - the largest positive-negative gap.

Traditional bank absence: Major banks like Wells Fargo only mentioned in regulatory exit contexts, suggesting AI models focus on specialist reverse mortgage providers rather than traditional banking institutions.

Gemini Divergence & Data Sources

Gemini's unique perspective: Only model to flag Finance of America negatively (worst reviews, consumer complaints) while other 5 models consistently praise it - suggesting different evaluation timeframes or source prioritization.

Methodology implications: Low consensus on negative assessments (17-33%) likely reflects different training data recency, source diversity, and risk evaluation criteria rather than analytical capability differences.

Missing traditional players: Absence of major banks except in exit scenarios suggests AI models may prioritize specialist industry sources over broader financial institution coverage.
