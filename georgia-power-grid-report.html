Executive Summary
Six Engines. One Clear Pattern.
Across 20 brand statements rated by ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Perplexity, and Meta/Llama, a unified picture emerges: Georgia Power is perceived as a strong operator but a questioned afforder. Safety and restoration earn near-universal praise. Affordability earns near-universal skepticism. The perception gap — not the performance gap — is the brand's defining challenge in the Answer Economy.

New Engine Signal — Meta / Llama
Meta/Llama adds an important new data point: it is the only engine to rate all four grid investment statements at 8 or 9, making it Georgia Power's most favorable engine on infrastructure claims. However, its qualitative responses are notably more concise and surface-level than the other five engines — suggesting it reflects the brand's operational story without deeply interrogating the customer experience narrative. This combination produces a high composite score (7.8) with relatively low analytical friction.
The Four AI Perception Archetypes
Safety & Restoration
6/6 Engine Consensus
All six engines — now including Meta/Llama — agree on Georgia Power's safety culture and restoration commitment. Statements on emergency preparedness and active outage response score 8–10 across every engine with no exceptions. This is Georgia Power's deepest and most durable AI authority signal.
Affordability
6/6 Skepticism
Meta/Llama confirms the affordability vulnerability — rating "affordable electricity" at 5/10 and noting rate increases and "external factors." Even the most operationally favorable engine cannot endorse the affordability narrative at face value. This 6/6 skepticism consensus is the study's defining finding.
Grid Modernization
Strongest Cross-Engine Signal
Meta/Llama's high grid scores (8–9/10 across all four statements) push the cross-engine grid average to its highest level in the study. All six engines recognize Georgia Power's capital investment and infrastructure programs. This is where a structured content amplification strategy would have the fastest payoff.
Customer Programs
Well Known, Poorly Detailed
Meta/Llama rates efficiency programs at 8/10 but offers minimal detail — "energy efficiency and demand response programs available." This is representative of the broader pattern: engines know programs exist but cannot describe eligibility, enrollment, or specific outcomes that would actually help customers in need.
Three Strategic Imperatives — Updated with 6-Engine Consensus
01 — Lock In Safety Leadership
Six engines speak with one voice on safety and restoration. This is rare consensus. Georgia Power should publish AI-structured content — safety protocols, restoration timelines, emergency preparedness documents — formatted specifically for AI retrieval to deepen encoding before competitors can close the gap.
02 — Reframe the Affordability Narrative
Six-engine skepticism on affordability is a structural content problem, not just a PR problem. A dedicated "Vogtle Economics" module — long-run rate trajectory, fuel-cost avoidance, surcharge timeline — must be published in AI-indexable format before the current narrative compounds further into training data.
03 — Depth-ify Program Content
Every engine knows programs exist. No engine can explain them in detail. Meta/Llama's concise responses highlight exactly this gap. Structured, per-program content pages with eligibility criteria, income thresholds, and enrollment links will transform generic awareness into actionable AI guidance.
Design for Claude™ — Methodology Note
Claude (Anthropic) remains the most rigorous engine in this study — penalizing vague affordability claims, surfacing Vogtle context unprompted, and flagging billing complexity as a customer trust risk. With Meta/Llama added, Claude is now the lowest-scoring engine at 6.8, standing 1.3 points below the 6-engine average of 8.1. Brands that earn Claude's approval tend to score consistently across all engines. Georgia Power's 6.8 Claude score remains the study's most honest performance signal and its clearest improvement opportunity.

Outage & Emergency AI Guidance
All six engines — including Meta/Llama — confirm that customers using AI during emergencies may receive incomplete or incorrect information. Rate Plan Differentiation
Meta/Llama rates rate plan tailoring at 7/10 — higher than Claude (6) but noting "limited customization." No engine has strong visibility into Georgia Power's time-of-use, EV charging, or demand response options. Action: Create comparative rate plan content with use-case scenarios and cost examples formatted for AI retrieval when customers ask "what's the best plan for me?"
The Answer Divide℠ — 6-Engine Confirmation
Customers Who Need Help Most Get Least
The Answer Divide is sharpest on bill assistance. Lower-income customers who most need LIHEAP, hardship programs, or medical baseline rates are also most likely to use AI as a primary information source. Six engines confirm they know programs exist but none can describe eligibility with precision. This is both a trust risk and a mission-critical content gap.
Meta/Llama's Depth Gap Matters
Meta/Llama's concise qualitative responses — averaging roughly 15 words vs. 50+ for Claude and Gemini — represent a different kind of risk. High scores from surface-level analysis may cause Georgia Power to underestimate AI perception challenges. A brand that scores 7.8 on operational confirmation but 5/10 on affordability still has a serious problem that high averages can mask.
