How to Use Earned Media Sourcing: Health
A quick guide for brand managers, PR strategists, and media planners
What You're Looking At
↑ Y-Axis: Answer Impact
How strongly this publication influences what AI engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity) say about health brands and topics. Higher = more AI citation authority.
→ X-Axis: Accessibility
How easy it is to get coverage or place content — factoring in editorial barriers, paid options, and relationship requirements. Right = easier to access.
⬤ Node Size = Answer Impact Score
Larger circles have higher AI citation scores. The number inside each node is a weighted priority score (Impact 65% + Accessibility 35%). Score 70+ = High Priority. Color = Use Case category.
The Four Quadrants
★ Prime Targets (top-right)
High impact + low barriers. Start here. Best ROI for most health brands — Healthline, WebMD, Verywell Health cluster here.
Build Relationships (top-left)
High impact + high barriers. Worth the long-term investment. Mayo, Harvard, NYT live here. Play the 12–18 month game.
Quick Wins (bottom-right)
Lower impact + accessible. Good for volume and supporting narratives. Well+Good, MBG, and Verywell Fit sit here.
Avoid (bottom-left)
Low impact + high barriers. Poor use of resources unless you have a very specific reason. Redirect budget elsewhere.
Step-by-Step Guide
  • 1Pick a Campaign Goal — Select a goal chip (AI Answer Coverage, Brand Validation, etc.) in the yellow bar. Your top recommended publications will pulse gold on the matrix.
  • 2Filter by Use Case — Use the colored chips (Education, Validation, Awareness, Authority) to isolate publication types relevant to your strategy.
  • 3Search by name — Type a publication name in the search bar to instantly highlight it on the matrix with a gold ring.
  • 4Click any node to open the intelligence brief — full description, audience, AI signal types, activation strategy, and contact path.
  • 5Compare two publications — After opening a brief, click "Compare with Another" to select a second publication and view them side by side.
  • 6Use the three tabs inside each brief — Intelligence (what it is), Strategy (how to activate), Contact (how to get in).
Score Explained
Priority Score Formula
Score = (Answer Impact × 0.65) + (Accessibility × 0.35)

Answer Impact is weighted more heavily because the primary goal is influencing what AI engines say — not just getting coverage. A 70+ score signals a high-priority publication that delivers strong ROI. A 55–69 score is medium priority — solid supporting role. Below 55 is opportunistic only.
