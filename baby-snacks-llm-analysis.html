The Depth Chart: What Rufus Analyzes

1. Product Catalog Signals

SKU Performance: Number of active listings, conversion rates, inventory availability, and historical sales velocity across product variations.

Content Quality: A+ content completeness, image quality scores, enhanced brand content, bullet point optimization, and keyword density.

Attribution Data: Parent-child ASIN relationships, brand registry verification, and catalog authority within baby food taxonomy.

2. Customer Intelligence

Review Ecosystem: Star rating distribution, verified purchase ratio, review recency, sentiment analysis, and keyword frequency in customer feedback.

Q&A Authority: Volume and quality of answered questions, manufacturer response rate, and community engagement metrics.

Return/Satisfaction Data: Return rates, replacement requests, and A-to-Z guarantee claims as inverse trust signals.

3. Shopping Behavior Patterns

Add-to-Cart Signals: Click-through rates from search to product page, add-to-cart velocity, and cart abandonment rates.

Purchase Intent: Repeat purchase rates, Subscribe & Save adoption, and basket affinity with complementary products.

Session Data: Time on page, image zoom behavior, comparison shopping patterns, and cross-brand evaluation sequences.

4. External Authority Markers

Brand Equity Signals: Search volume for brand terms, direct URL traffic, brand-specific query patterns, and off-Amazon mention velocity.

Certifications & Compliance: USDA Organic, Non-GMO Project, clean label certifications, FDA compliance history, and third-party testing documentation.

Expert Endorsements: Pediatrician recommendations, parenting influencer mentions, and industry award recognition indexed in Rufus knowledge base.

5. Competitive Context

Category Dominance: Share of voice within baby food subcategories, pricing position relative to competitors, and promotional frequency.

Substitution Patterns: When customers view this brand vs competitors, which products win the conversion, and at what frequency.

Market Share Proxies: Best seller rank history, category rank stability, and seasonal performance consistency.

6. Rufus-Specific Weighting

Query-Answer Fit: Semantic matching between customer question intent and product attribute alignment (e.g., "organic" query → certified organic products).

Confidence Thresholds: Rufus appears to prioritize brands with consistent signals across multiple data sources rather than single strong signals.

Recency Bias: Recent review velocity, fresh content updates, and current inventory health may carry higher weight than historical performance alone.
