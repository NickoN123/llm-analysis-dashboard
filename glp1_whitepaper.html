Executive Summary
The New Gatekeepers of Drug Brand Narrative

When a patient, physician, or payer asks an AI engine about GLP-1 medications — Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound — the answer they receive is not random. It is assembled from a specific, mappable universe of sources. BrandRank's GLP-1 Source Index is the first comprehensive audit of that universe: cataloging every major domain feeding the AI answer layer across Rank in Search, Content Readiness, and Brand Vulnerability modules.

  • Medical reference sites — Healthline, Mayo Clinic, WebMD, Cleveland Clinic — dominate LLM citations. These independent platforms have become the de facto credibility layer for AI-generated drug information.
  • 54% of all Rank in Search citations come from HIGH authority sources. 89% are classified as INDEPENDENT — not owned by or affiliated with any tracked pharmaceutical brand.
  • Gemini cites the most diverse source set (6,609 unique URLs). Perplexity is most selective (2,185) but cross-cites heavily with Anthropic and Grok — suggesting a curated, overlapping source consensus.
  • Novo Nordisk's own domain (novonordisk.com) ranks in the top 5 for Brand Vulnerability sourcing — a rare example of brand-owned content directly shaping AI accuracy assessments.
  • The ozempic.com official website is the single most-cited individual source in vulnerability analysis — appearing across phrase, promise, statement, and SWOT categories simultaneously.
  • Domains like diabetes.org, fda.gov, and healthline.com appear across all three BRAIDHA modules, confirming their structural dominance in the GLP-1 AI information ecosystem.
Context & Methodology
Mapping the GLP-1 Information Ecosystem

BrandRank's BRAIDHA platform continuously queries six major AI answer engines — Gemini, OpenAI, Meta AI, Anthropic, Grok, and Perplexity — against a library of 326 GLP-1-related search terms. For each response, it logs the sources cited, classifies each by authority (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) and affiliation (INDEPENDENT / COMPETING / OWNED), and maps them against three analytical lenses.

Key Findings
Six Things Every GLP-1 Marketer Should Know
Finding 01
Healthline Tops the Citation Index
Healthline leads all domains with 1,228 unique URLs cited and 23,986 total LLM citations. Mayo Clinic (1,064 URLs) and GoodRx (1,028) round out the top three — giving consumer health media a dominant grip on AI-generated GLP-1 answers.
Finding 02
54% High Authority — But Pharma Brands Are Nearly Absent
Over half of all LLM citations originate from HIGH authority sources. Yet 89% are classified as INDEPENDENT. Pharmaceutical brand-owned content accounts for a small fraction of the total — a significant gap in brand-controlled narrative.
Finding 03
Gemini Casts the Widest Net
Gemini draws on 6,609 unique source URLs — nearly three times Perplexity's 2,185. OpenAI (5,372) and Meta AI (5,118) are mid-range. LLM source diversity varies dramatically and has direct implications for brand visibility strategy.
Finding 04
Reuters and FDA Lead Vulnerability Sourcing
In Brand Vulnerability, the top sources shift sharply toward news (reuters.com, cnbc.com) and regulatory (fda.gov, accessdata.fda.gov) domains. Scientific journals (NEJM, JAMA, PubMed) are also prominent — signaling AI accuracy checks are anchored in clinical and regulatory evidence.
Finding 05
Ozempic.com Is Its Own Biggest Vulnerability Source
The official Ozempic website is the single most-referenced individual URL in the Vulnerability module (720 total references), appearing across phrase, promise, statement, and SWOT categories. Brand-owned content is being actively used to evaluate brand accuracy — for better or worse.
Finding 06
Cross-Module Anchors Define the Ecosystem
Nine domains — including diabetes.org, fda.gov, healthline.com, and goodrx.com — appear consistently across all three BRAIDHA modules. These are the structural load-bearing sources of the GLP-1 AI narrative. Influencing them is the highest-leverage content strategy available.

Strategic Implications
What This Means for GLP-1 Brand Strategy

The source map is also a strategy map. Knowing which domains shape AI answers — and how — tells brand teams exactly where to focus their content, partnership, and monitoring efforts.

🎯
Target the Independent Gatekeepers
Healthline, Mayo Clinic, GoodRx, and WebMD collectively dominate the AI citation layer. For GLP-1 brands, earning accurate, favorable coverage on these platforms is more valuable than any owned content strategy.
🔬
Optimize for Vulnerability Sources
FDA databases, NIH/PubMed, diabetes.org, and Reuters form the factual verification backbone. If your brand's clinical claims are misrepresented here, AI will misrepresent them everywhere. Accuracy at these sources is non-negotiable.
📄
Structure Brand-Owned Content for AI
Novonordisk.com's Vulnerability ranking proves that brand-owned content can influence AI accuracy — if structured correctly. Content Readiness scoring reveals where brand pages fail to meet AI consumption standards.
🤖
Develop Engine-Specific Strategies
Gemini's 6,609 unique citations vs. Perplexity's 2,185 suggests fundamentally different retrieval approaches. GLP-1 visibility strategies must be differentiated by engine — not treated as a single channel.
🔗
Monitor Cross-Module Anchors
Domains appearing across all three modules are the highest-leverage assets in the ecosystem. Regular auditing of diabetes.org, fda.gov, and healthline.com coverage should be standard practice for any GLP-1 brand team.
📊
Track the Ownership Gap
At ~4% owned source affiliation, GLP-1 brands are nearly invisible in their own AI narrative. Closing this gap — through structured content, schema optimization, and citation-worthy assets — is the defining content challenge of the Answer Economy era.
